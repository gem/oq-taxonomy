Masonry construction where masonry walls are first laid and then horizontal and vertical reinforced concrete confining elements are cast. In this type of construction the concrete bonds to the masonry and the small-size columns and beams (called tie-columns and tie-beams) confine masonry wall panels. Confined masonry can be classified as the Wall lateral load-resisting system since the masonry bears gravity and lateral loads and the slender columns and beams do not constitute rigid frames but rather function as confining members. Construction where columns and beams are constructed before the masonry walls are laid is classified as an Infilled Flat Slab/Plate.
